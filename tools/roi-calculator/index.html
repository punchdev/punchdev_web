Ramp Time Costs

New SDRs take 3-6 months to reach full productivity. During ramp, they generate 50% fewer meetings while earning full salary. We calculate lost opportunity cost at $500 per missed meeting.

This reflects the average value of a qualified meeting that could have been booked by a fully-ramped SDR.

Turnover Impact

Average SDR turnover is 25-35% annually (Bridge Group, 2024). Replacement costs include recruiting fees (50% of base salary), lost productivity during ramp, and knowledge loss.

What's included in the SDR cost calculation?

We calculate the true fully-loaded cost of an SDR, not just base salary:

  • Base salary (location-adjusted)
  • Benefits (25% of salary: health, 401k, payroll taxes)
  • Ramp costs (lost productivity during 1-6 month ramp period)
  • Turnover costs (recruiting, training, knowledge loss)
  • Tools & software ($200/mo: CRM, engagement platform, data)
  • Management overhead (if team >3 SDRs, proportional manager cost)

We do NOT include desk space, onboarding costs, or commission (since that's performance-based and applies to both SDR and AI models).

Why is turnover such a big cost factor?

SDR turnover averages 25-35% annually, which means:

  • You lose a fully-ramped SDR every 3-4 years (on average)
  • Replacement costs ~50% of base salary in recruiting fees alone
  • 3-6 months of lost productivity during new hire ramp
  • Knowledge and relationship loss with prospects

For a $70K SDR with 25% turnover and 4-month ramp, you're paying an extra $20K+ annually just from churn. This compounds with larger teams.

Does AI actually work as well as human SDRs?

This is the most common objection we hear. The short answer: it depends on what you're optimizing for.

AI excels at:

  • Volume prospecting (100s-1000s of personalized emails per month)
  • Consistent quality (no "bad days" or performance variance)
  • Speed (instant reply handling, 24/7 operation)
  • Data-driven optimization (A/B testing every variable)

Human SDRs excel at:

  • Complex objection handling and nuanced conversations
  • Building relationships over long sales cycles
  • Adapting to highly dynamic/emerging markets
  • White-glove, fully custom outreach (5-10 touches per prospect)

Most B2B SaaS companies find that AI can handle 70-80% of outbound prospecting while human SDRs focus on high-value, high-touch accounts. This is often the best of both worlds.
